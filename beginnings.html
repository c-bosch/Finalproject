How C&C Began

hint: it starts with two very bored illustration majors

In the spring of their freshman year, Christine and Cathy, two illustration majors at Washinton University, found them selves on the same freshman dorm floor and the same freshman required courses. The stab-your-eye-with-a-fork kind of required courses. In their case, this took the form of Modern Art History. Normally you might think, "Hey! Modern art! That's interesting right?" This is what they thought as well. They were so very, very wrong.
Yet in the terrible crusible of 90 minute monotone lectures, a brilliant idea was born. Cathy and Christine began passing a sketchbook back and forth, creating communal doodles which soon became a joint commic strip that continued far beyond Art History class. The premise of the stip has changed many times since that semester, primarily centering around their failing love lives and mutual friends, but the concept of two artist, one story remains.
These pages here are those early doodles that Christine found in a sketchbook under her bed while looking for a lost sock. Hope they amuse you as much as they amused their creators.
